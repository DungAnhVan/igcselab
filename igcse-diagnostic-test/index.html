Why diagnostic first?

Before more lessons, understand the pattern behind the mistakes.

Many students practise harder but improve slowly because they are repeating work around the real gap. The diagnostic session checks how the student thinks through questions, uses formulas, reads graphs, handles units, and responds to command words.

What we check

Academic gaps that usually hide behind the final answer

1

Core concepts

Whether the student understands the idea behind the procedure.

2

Algebra and calculation

Accuracy, rearranging formulas, substitutions, and multi-step working.

3

Graph reading

Interpreting gradients, intercepts, trends, axes, and data-based questions.

4

Units and formulas

Choosing formulas, checking units, and avoiding common conversion errors.

5

Command words

Knowing the difference between describe, explain, calculate, compare, and evaluate.

6

Exam technique

Working style, mark allocation, showing steps, and time awareness.

7

Reasoning

How the student explains cause, evidence, method, and conclusion.

8

Past-paper readiness

Whether the student can transfer knowledge into exam-style questions.

FAQ

Common questions about the IGCSE Diagnostic Test

Is the diagnostic test a full mock exam?

No. It is a focused diagnostic session designed to reveal weak points in concepts, calculations, reasoning, and exam technique.

Does my child need to join tutoring after the diagnostic?

No long-term course commitment is required. Parents receive a roadmap first and can decide the next step afterwards.

Which subjects can be checked?

IGCSE Lab can check Math, Physics, Chemistry, and Combined Science skills depending on the student's needs.

Can the session be online?

Yes. Online diagnostic sessions are suitable for most students. Offline options can be discussed based on location and availability.
